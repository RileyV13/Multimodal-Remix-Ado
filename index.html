Her Journey

Timeline

From bedroom recordings to world stages — the story of Ado's rise.

2019 – 2020

DS Recordings & Vocaloid Covers

Ado began uploading cover songs from her bedroom — singing over beloved Vocaloid tracks. Her raw, powerful voice immediately stood out online.

October 2020

Debut: "Usseewa"

Ado's debut single exploded onto the scene, hitting tens of millions of streams. Her masked identity and emotionally charged voice immediately fascinated Japan and beyond.

2021 – 2022

Big Hit Songs & Events

A string of viral singles followed — Kyoufuu All Back, Odo, Shinjuu — cementing Ado's reputation as one of Japan's most exciting voices of a generation.

January 2022

First Album: "Kyoufuu All Back"

Her debut album charted across Japan, bringing together her best early work and introducing her to a wider global audience.

August 2022

One Piece Film: Red

Ado voiced "Uta," a fictional pop star in the film, and performed all songs for the character. "New Genesis" became a global phenomenon, introducing Ado to millions of anime fans worldwide.

2023

Song Covers Album & Wish World Tour

Released a celebrated covers album revisiting classic Vocaloid and J-pop songs. Then launched the Wish World Tour — her first international run, selling out venues across Asia, Europe, and North America.

2024

Second Album & Hibana World Tour

Her second studio album arrived to massive acclaim. The Hibana World Tour followed, spanning multiple continents and confirming her status as a true global artist.

2025

Vivarium

Her most recent release — a lush, cinematic single with a visually stunning music video. Vivarium showcases a new artistic maturity and emotional depth.

April 2026

KIRA Upcoming

Who Is She?

About Ado

J-Pop J-Rock Electropop Vocaloid Utaite Alt-Pop One Piece Live Performer

Ado is a Japanese singer known for her powerful, genre-blending voice and her carefully guarded anonymous identity.

Born in 2002, she began uploading cover songs on YouTube while still a teenager, instantly gaining attention for her raw, emotive delivery on technically demanding songs from the Vocaloid world.

Her debut single "Usseewa" shattered Japanese streaming records and announced a singular new artist. She has never publicly revealed her face — performing behind a screen at live shows — making her music and voice the complete center of attention.

Inspired by the Vocaloid community, she credits producers like syudou, Utata-P, and Kenshi Yonezu for shaping her sound. Fans around the world connect to her work because she gives voice to things people feel but struggle to say.

Her journey — from a closet studio to Lollapalooza — is a story about what happens when pure artistic honesty finds the right audience.
